Where's Waldo Level 1 - Beach Festival

Waldo arrives at a packed seaside festival hoping for a quiet afternoon. The problem is, everyone here seems to love red-and-white as much as he does. People are swimming, sunbathing, shouting for ice cream, building sandcastles, and crowding every corner of the shore.

The beach is full of bright towels, colorful umbrellas, and hundreds of people doing all sorts of things. Some are playing volleyball near the water. Others are lying on the sand reading books. Kids are running around with buckets and spades. Lifeguards watch from their towers. And somewhere in the middle of all this fun, Waldo is standing with his arms at his side, waiting to be found.

Odlaw slipped into the crowd the moment they reached the beach, already plotting some trick of his own. His yellow-and-black stripes should be easy to see, but the beach has so many patterns and colors that he blends in better than you would think.

Wizard Whitebeard vanished before Waldo even noticed. His long beard and star-covered robe are somewhere out there among the swimmers and sunbathers.

Characters to Find in Level 1

  • Waldo — The main character. He wears a red-and-white striped shirt, blue jeans, and a matching bobble hat. He also has round glasses.
  • Odlaw — Waldo's sneaky rival. He wears yellow-and-black stripes and always has a mischievous look on his face.
  • Wizard Whitebeard — A wise old man with a very long white beard, wearing a tall pointed hat and blue robe covered in stars.

Tips for Finding Everyone in Level 1

  • Watch out for red-and-white tricks: The beach is full of striped towels, umbrellas, and swimwear that look just like Waldo's shirt. Do not click too fast.
  • Look for Waldo's hat first: His round bobble hat often sticks up above the crowd. It is easier to spot than his full body.
  • Find Odlaw by looking for yellow-and-black: His colors are different from the beach theme, so he might be the easiest to find.
  • Check near the edges: Characters often hide near the borders of the scene where players do not look right away.
  • Use zoom carefully: Zoom in to check areas closely, but zoom out often so you do not miss the bigger picture.
